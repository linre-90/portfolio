Goal of this project was to showcase strengths in HDRP rendering pipeline.

Small room connected to hallway

Music by Music_Unlimited from Pixabay

What is it?

This was a school assignment, and we were supposed to pick either URP or HDRP render pipeline and showcase its strength. I am fairly familiar with URP and decided to try out HDRP and what I can achieve with it. Scene uses a lot of shiny and transparent surfaces, so I decided to put ray tracing power to SSR reflection. I also implemented DLSS with different profiles and scene ran with DirectX12.

Every single item in the project is modeled and textured by me using Blender and AmbienCG/Unsplash as texture sources.

The result

I am very happy with the result. The scene looks great to my eyes, and it is pretty good even with really heavy post-processing. The DX12 is really, really heavy compared to DX11. My computer hovered somewhere around 30-40 FPS without DLSS. DLSS features are toggleable at runtime so the difference can be seen instantly. DLSS gives me about 20-30 fps boost with maximum quality. Some profiles effect more and some have a negative impact on performance.

Windows were made with transparent paintings

DLSS

In the hallways, ceiling lights had very small moving animations and there were dust particles. The main issue that I have with DLSS is that those small animations disappear if the camera stays still, but otherwise, it is a really promising technique, especially in scenes that have rapid movement.
